Welcome to my homepage. I am a graduate student, working with Marco Baroni on compositional distributional semantics at the CLIC Lab of the Center for Mind/Brain Sciences of the University of Trento, Italy (map).

My research

My research interest lies in the area of NLP, and currently, I have been working on how to obtain the meaning of phrases and sentences through compositional distributional semantics models. So far, I have been investigating both linguistically motivated models, which are inspired by the function-argument view of formal semantics, as well as straightforward but empirically stable models. The parameters of these models are learned through unsupervised objectives such as predicting the surrounding contexts of phrases and sentences, using a variety of methods ranging from simple least square to deep neural networks. Given the encouraging results of these models, I am planning to improve the models with a multi-task learning framework.
Recently, I also take a step into the multi-modal field by looking into how information from other modalities such as visual features helps in learning word embeddings.
